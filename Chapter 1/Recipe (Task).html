15-Minute Tuna Pasta

Looking for a speedy meal solution? Canned tuna and pasta has long been my reliable choice when hunger strikes unexpectedly. This dish is straightforward, quick to prepare, and requires just 15 minutes of your time.

What you will need

    Ingredients

  • Pasta (can be preferred choice of pasta)
  • Oil (can be preferred choice of oil)
  • Garlic
  • Canned Tuna
  • Lemon Juice
  • Parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to make tuna pasta

  1. Begin by bringing a pot of water to a boil and cooking the pasta until it reaches al dente texture, following the instructions on the package.
  2. While the pasta is cooking, heat a tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Once the oil is hot, add two cloves of minced garlic and saut√© for about 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to let it burn.
  4. Next, stir in a can of drained tuna, the juice of one lemon, and a handful of freshly chopped parsley. Allow the flavors to meld together for a couple of minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. When the pasta is done cooking, drain it well and add it to the skillet with the tuna mixture. Toss everything together until the pasta is well coated in the sauce.
  6. Season the dish generously with salt and pepper, adjusting to taste as needed. You can also add a pinch of red pepper flakes for a hint of heat if desired.
  7. Serve your delicious tuna and pasta dish hot, garnished with additional parsley or grated Parmesan cheese if desired. Enjoy your quick and satisfying meal!
